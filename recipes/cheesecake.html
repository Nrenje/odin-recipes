Basque Burnt Cheesecake

Basque Burnt Cheesecake is a crustless, caramelized cheesecake from San Sebastián, Spain. It’s intentionally baked at a high temperature for a deeply browned, slightly bitter top and a creamy, custard-like center. Simple yet elegant, it’s rich, fluffy, and irresistibly smooth — the perfect dessert for any occasion.

Ingredients

Cheesecake Batter

  • 500 g cream cheese (softened)
  • 200 g granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 250 ml heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Pan

  • Butter (for greasing)
  • Parliament paper (for lining the pan)

Procedure

  • Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Lightly grease a 9-inch springform pan and line it with parchment paper, making sure the paper extends above the rim to hold the rising batter.
  • In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar together until completely smooth and creamy, about 2–3 minutes.
  • Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Scrape down the sides of the bowl to ensure even mixing.
  • Pour in the heavy cream, vanilla extract, and salt. Continue mixing until smooth and silky.
  • Sift in the flour and gently whisk until just combined. Do not overmix, as this can affect the texture.
  • Pour the batter into the prepared pan and tap it lightly on the counter to remove air bubbles.
  • Bake for 45–55 minutes, until the top is deeply browned and the center is still slightly jiggly when shaken.
  • Remove from the oven and let it cool completely in the pan. The cake will deflate slightly as it cools, creating its signature rustic look.
  • Chill for at least 4 hours or overnight before slicing for the best texture. Serve slightly cold or at room temperature for a creamy, melt-in-your-mouth experience.
